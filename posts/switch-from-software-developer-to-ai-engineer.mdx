description: "I gave up an SDE2 promotion to switch to AI. Here's why, and what actually changed."

---

so it started in 2023. i was not feeling good at all about the things i was doing.

let me explain. i started development in 2018 because it was cool. it gave me that kick, that feeling of happiness. then i started making money from it and i was like, wow, this is monetizable. i like it.

## the plateau

when i entered corporate, everything was new. what we see on the internet and how things work in reality are completely different. but i was full of hopes and energy. i learned everything.

then as i started becoming a senior engineer, i realized i had outgrown the kind of problems i was solving. getting into calls, building similar kinds of apps. don't get me wrong, the work was solid and i learned a lot. but the restless side of me was craving something new.

i started reading about complex architectures. kubernetes, azure, aws. it was fun. but after that, what now? hello darkness my old friend.

then i got into real-time manufacturing data. hard problems. learning the business side, thinking from a technical aspect, then implementing solutions. i loved it. but after a while, the same feeling came back. i needed that rush. something that would make me lose my mind.

strangely, i was getting dopamine hits from these kinds of problems. solving them gave me a high. but i wanted more.

## the search for something more

so i went wide. blockchain, webrtc, websockets, ruby, mobile development. i built apps on webrtc, recreated the gather town app from scratch, created chat apps, built mobile apps.

still not satisfied.

by that time ai was booming and i had done some courses on it.

## the moment it clicked

then in november 2024, i was given a task to build a poc on natural language to sql.

damn. that was the project that made me feel like *this is the place i want to be*.

i built that and got curious. how do these models work? how were they built? i take 2 hours to write some apis, models are doing it in seconds. HOWWW?

then i read about rag, multi-agent systems. rabbit hole after rabbit hole.

## the choice

then there was an opening in my company for an ai engineer role. i reached out to my current manager, gave the interview, did the case study. he selected me.

but here's the twist. i was about to get a senior promotion. i had to choose: SDE2 or AI Engineer.

a couple of friends told me, "bhai paisa dekh, SDE2 makes more than AI Engineers."

but i never wanted to be an engineer for name's sake. i did it purely out of curiosity. to understand how systems are built. how search works, how a terminal was built. that kind of thing.

so i made the decision and moved to the ai role.

## what actually changed

to be honest, i was scared at first. but damn bro, this field is so so good.

i'm learning something new every day. sometimes 4-5 things a day. and the experience i had as a developer is helping me build scalable, production-ready apps. the business knowledge helps me understand the problem even more.

now i know ai and business and sdlc. i'm like a full package. building new things, shipping them like anything.

overall, this was a good decision.

## should you switch?

if someone asks me, "hrushi, should i move to an ai role?"

i'd ask, are you open to changes? can you build on any framework?

if yes, are you willing to put in hours and break your head almost every day?

if yes, then this is the field you should be in. every day, new discovery. you are always learning. and the community is super open and helpful.
